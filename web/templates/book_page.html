Milk and Honey

Author: Rupi Kaur

Shared by: Helena Mance




Elena Cai
|
January 15, 2021
I’m very grateful that I received this book. When I saw this book, I didn't think it would bring me such a big touch. It was mailed to my home in a parcel. The black cover was written with simple five words: milk and honey. Milk and honey. It's that simple. What it is about is hard to say in one sentence. About love, about healing, about finding self, and about the meaning of life. Each page is a small poem, or a short essay. From suffering pain to self-healing, the author described her psychological journey with simple brush strokes, and also created a new impression of poetry. It reads very smoothly, but each sentence is meaningful.

After reading this book for the first time, I felt that its text was really explicit. After half a while, I found that between the lines of text, the chants and shouts about love and life that the author wanted to convey, and that was too exaggerated.
